1. Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive and balanced analysis of compliance status at Quenby Rest Home, highlighting both areas of strength and those requiring attention based on CQC regulations. The analysis incorporates findings from the CQC inspection conducted on 20th March 2025 by inspectors Deborah and Gaynor Chamberlain. This inspection was triggered by a safeguarding concern and investigation into an unexpected death of a resident who was admitted to Quenby Rest Home as part of the "Return to Home" contract with Essex County Council - a program providing temporary 4-week enablement placements for five residents before discharge to their own homes.

The initial CQC inspection was conducted to assess compliance with fundamental standards of care as required by the Health and Social Care Act 2008 (Regulated Activities) Regulations 2014. This was followed by a focused follow-up inspection on 1st April 2025 to assess progress against the Section 29A Warning Notice issued following the initial January inspection. The inspection process involved comprehensive review of care documentation, staff interviews, observations of care delivery, and detailed assessment of safety protocols, particularly those related to temporary placements.

The report analysis is based on 348 documents comprising over 2,200,000 words of policy and procedural documentation, staff records, incident reports, resident care plans, and internal audit records provided by Cathena. Particular attention has been given to the documentation and processes surrounding the "Return to Home" contract residents, with additional scrutiny of enablement services, risk assessments, and discharge planning procedures.

Overall compliance score across all frameworks is 76.2%, indicating a Medium Risk level that requires targeted remediation in several key areas, while acknowledging the significant positive work already being undertaken. The report specifically recognizes the comprehensive care plan audits and quality assurance processes implemented by Cathena as evidence of commitment to continuous improvement. There has been substantial progress since the warning notice issued in January 2025, with notable improvements in medication management (+27.2%) and governance framework (+25.3%).

Alongside these positive initiatives, 4 areas requiring further attention were identified, particularly in nutritional needs (REG_14), complaints handling (REG_16), staffing (REG_19), and duty of candour (REG_20). This report provides detailed analysis of both strengths and improvement opportunities, along with comprehensive visualisation of compliance status across multiple frameworks. An actionable remediation plan with clear prioritisation is included for areas requiring enhancement, building upon the existing quality foundations.

7. Root Cause Analysis

This section provides a detailed analysis of the four priority areas identified as requiring attention, examining root causes and suggesting remediation approaches based on evidence from the documentation review.

REG_14: Nutritional Needs (68.7% Compliance)

Root Causes:

  • Incomplete nutritional assessments for 8 out of 26 residents reviewed
  • Inconsistent monitoring of food and fluid intake for residents at risk
  • Absence of systematic weight monitoring schedule in 12 care plans
  • Limited evidence of dietitian input for residents with complex nutritional needs
  • Staff knowledge gaps regarding management of specific nutritional conditions

Recommended Actions:

  • Implement standardized nutritional assessment tools for all residents
  • Develop clear protocols for food and fluid monitoring with threshold for intervention
  • Establish routine weight monitoring schedule with escalation pathways
  • Formalize arrangements for dietitian referrals and input
  • Provide targeted training on nutritional care for all relevant staff

REG_16: Complaints (67.2% Compliance)

Root Causes:

  • Inconsistent application of complaints procedure with delays in responses noted
  • Limited evidence of systematic analysis of complaints to identify patterns
  • Unclear documentation of actions taken in response to complaints
  • Resident/family feedback indicates low awareness of complaints process
  • No clear mechanism for sharing learning from complaints

Recommended Actions:

  • Revise complaints procedure to include clear timelines and responsibilities
  • Implement quarterly analysis of complaints to identify trends and patterns
  • Develop standardized documentation format for complaint responses
  • Enhance resident/family information about complaints process
  • Establish "learning from complaints" as standing item in staff meetings

REG_19: Fit & Proper Persons (69.8% Compliance)

Root Causes:

  • Inconsistent application of recruitment checks for staff and volunteers
  • Incomplete documentation of qualifications verification for 4 staff members
  • Gaps in DBS check renewal process with 3 checks overdue
  • Limited evidence of ongoing fitness assessment for existing staff
  • Absence of formal process for checking professional registrations

Recommended Actions:

  • Implement comprehensive recruitment checklist for all new starters
  • Establish central register for tracking qualification verification
  • Develop automated reminder system for DBS check renewals
  • Implement annual fitness declarations for all staff
  • Create formal process for regular verification of professional registrations

REG_20: Duty of Candour (64.5% Compliance)

Root Causes:

  • Inconsistent understanding of duty of candour requirements among staff
  • Limited evidence of formal duty of candour notifications following incidents
  • Absence of clear documentation template for duty of candour communications
  • No systematic process for identifying notifiable safety incidents
  • Lack of training specific to duty of candour requirements

Recommended Actions:

  • Develop comprehensive duty of candour policy and procedure
  • Create standardized template for duty of candour communications
  • Implement process for identifying notifiable safety incidents
  • Provide targeted training on duty of candour for all relevant staff
  • Establish monitoring system for duty of candour compliance

9. Conclusion and Recommendations

This comprehensive analysis demonstrates that Quenby Rest Home has established robust systems in many areas of regulatory compliance, with particularly strong performance in Health & Safety (93.3%), Medication Management (87.6%), and Resident Experience (84.2%). These areas represent significant strengths upon which the organization can build.

However, the analysis has also identified four priority areas requiring focused attention: Nutritional Needs (68.7%), Complaints (67.2%), Fit & Proper Persons (69.8%), and Duty of Candour (64.5%). These areas represent the greatest regulatory risk and should be prioritized for immediate action.

The recommended approach involves a combination of policy development, training enhancement, and implementation of robust monitoring systems. By addressing these areas systematically through the proposed timeline, Quenby Rest Home can significantly improve its overall compliance position and, more importantly, enhance the quality and safety of care provided to residents.

Key recommendations for the organization are:

  1. Prioritize immediate risks: Address the highest risk elements within identified gaps, particularly focusing on completing overdue DBS checks and reviewing nutritional assessments for all residents.
  2. Develop comprehensive policies and procedures: Ensure all priority areas have clear, detailed policies and procedures that align with regulatory requirements and best practice.
  3. Enhance staff knowledge and skills: Provide targeted training to address knowledge gaps, particularly in relation to nutritional care and duty of candour.
  4. Implement robust monitoring systems: Establish systems to monitor compliance on an ongoing basis, with clear escalation pathways for identified issues.
  5. Foster a culture of continuous improvement: Build on existing quality assurance processes to embed a culture of continuous improvement throughout the organization.

By implementing these recommendations, Quenby Rest Home will be well-positioned to achieve and maintain regulatory compliance, while continuing to enhance the quality of care provided to residents.

Appendix A: Requirements

This appendix provides detailed information about the regulatory requirements referenced in this report, including specific guidance and compliance criteria.

REG_14: Meeting Nutritional and Hydration Needs

Summary: The intention of this regulation is to ensure that people who use services have adequate nutrition and hydration to sustain life and good health and reduce the risks of malnutrition and dehydration while they receive care and treatment.

Key Requirements:

  • Providers must ensure people have enough to eat and drink to meet their nutrition and hydration needs and receive the support they need to do so.
  • Providers must make sure that the food and drink they provide is nutritionally balanced and supports the person's health and well-being.
  • People's food and drink intake must be monitored to identify where support is needed.
  • Providers must follow medical advice about dietary requirements and preferences, including those relating to cultural, religious, or health needs.

REG_16: Receiving and Acting on Complaints

Summary: The intention of this regulation is to ensure that people can make a complaint about their care and treatment. To meet this regulation, providers must have an effective system in place to identify, receive, record, handle and respond to complaints.

Key Requirements:

  • Providers must have a complaints system in place and must investigate complaints in a thorough and timely manner.
  • Providers must take necessary and proportionate action in response to any failure identified by a complaint.
  • Providers must ensure that complainants do not face discrimination, harassment, or disadvantage as a result of making a complaint.
  • Providers must securely maintain a record of all complaints, including details of investigations and actions taken.

REG_19: Fit and Proper Persons Employed

Summary: The intention of this regulation is to ensure that providers only employ 'fit and proper' staff who are able to provide care and treatment appropriate to their role and to enable them to provide the regulated activity.

Key Requirements:

  • Providers must operate robust recruitment procedures, including undertaking any relevant checks (such as DBS checks where appropriate).
  • Staff must have the qualifications, competence, skills and experience necessary for the work to be performed.
  • Providers must ensure that staff are physically and mentally fit for the work they are employed to do.
  • Providers must have appropriate systems for ongoing monitoring of staff fitness and competency.

REG_20: Duty of Candour

Summary: The intention of this regulation is to ensure that providers are open and transparent with people who use services and other 'relevant persons' in relation to care and treatment. It also sets out specific requirements that providers must follow when things go wrong with care and treatment.

Key Requirements:

  • Providers must act in an open and transparent way with relevant persons in relation to care and treatment provided to people who use services.
  • When a notifiable safety incident occurs, the registered person must inform the relevant person, provide reasonable support, provide truthful information, and an apology.
  • Providers must keep a written record of all communications with the relevant person under this regulation.
  • Staff must be trained in and understand their responsibilities under this regulation.
