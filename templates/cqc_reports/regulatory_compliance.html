Inspection Findings and Regulatory Breaches

The inspection identified several areas of non-compliance with CQC regulatory standards, resulting in a formal warning notice under Section 29A of the Health and Social Care Act 2008 and requirement notices for multiple regulations.

Section 29A Warning Notice (Issued 25 January 2025)

Warning Reference: WN-12534-QHRF

Response Deadline: 25 February 2025

The warning notice identified significant concerns regarding the governance of the service, particularly in relation to:

  • Inadequate oversight of medication administration and management
  • Failure to ensure robust safeguarding procedures were followed
  • Lack of effective quality assurance systems
  • Insufficient staff supervision and competency assessments

Document Reference: WN-QHRF-012534 | Issued by: Sarah Thompson, CQC Inspector

Regulatory Breaches

Regulation 12: Safe Care and Treatment

Compliance Rating: Non-compliant

Key Findings:

  • Inconsistent medication administration practices with 5 instances of medication errors identified during inspection
  • Incomplete risk assessments for 8 out of 26 residents reviewed
  • Inadequate infection control measures in the medication room and dining areas
  • Lack of regular review of care plans for residents with complex needs

CQC Inspector Comments: "During our inspection, we found medication administration records (MARs) were not always completed accurately, with gaps in recording for 6 residents. Staff were unable to demonstrate consistent knowledge of medication procedures, particularly for controlled drugs."

Regulation 13: Safeguarding

Compliance Rating: Partially Compliant

Key Findings:

  • Inconsistent application of safeguarding procedures
  • Delays in reporting safeguarding concerns to the local authority (3 instances identified)
  • Inadequate documentation of safeguarding investigations
  • Staff demonstrated varied understanding of safeguarding procedures during interviews

CQC Inspector Comments: "While staff could describe how to recognise abuse, the documentation and reporting of safeguarding concerns was inconsistent. Three incidents that should have been reported as safeguarding concerns had not been appropriately escalated."

Regulation 17: Good Governance

Compliance Rating: Non-compliant

Key Findings:

  • Ineffective quality assurance systems failing to identify and address risks
  • Audit processes not robust enough to drive improvement
  • Lack of oversight of key service areas including medication, safeguarding and staff supervision
  • Insufficient analysis of incidents and accidents to identify patterns and trends

CQC Inspector Comments: "The governance systems in place were not effective in identifying and addressing risks. Audits were completed but actions arising from these were not consistently followed through. There was limited evidence that learning from incidents was embedded into practice."

Regulation 18: Staffing

Compliance Rating: Partially Compliant

Key Findings:

  • Staff supervision not occurring at the required frequency (only 68% of staff had received supervision within the previous 3 months)
  • Training compliance below the provider's target of 90% in key areas including safeguarding (76%), moving and handling (72%), and mental capacity (68%)
  • Limited evidence of competency assessments for medication administration
  • Inconsistent understanding of policies and procedures among staff

CQC Inspector Comments: "Staff told us they felt supported but records showed that formal supervision was not taking place at the frequency specified in the provider's policy. Training compliance was below the provider's target in several key areas."
