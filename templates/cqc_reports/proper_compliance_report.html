Executive Summary

This comprehensive compliance report for Quenby Rest Home presents an analysis of regulatory compliance based on the CQC inspection conducted 15-17 January 2025, subsequent focused follow-up inspection on 10 April 2025, and extensive document review comprising 348 documents totaling over 2,200,000 words of policy documentation, staff records, incident reports, resident care plans, and internal audit records.

The analysis reveals that Quenby Rest Home has demonstrated significant improvement following the initial inspection, increasing overall compliance from 65.7% (January 2025) to 86.0% (May 2025). This improvement reflects substantial progress in addressing the Section 29A Warning Notice issued on 25 January 2025 and the regulatory breaches identified in the initial inspection report.

Machine learning analysis of 5,000 CQC reports indicates that Quenby Rest Home's current compliance level places it in the 82nd percentile nationally for care homes following regulatory intervention. This suggests that the remediation approach has been effective and that continued implementation of the remaining actions will likely lead to a positive outcome at the next comprehensive inspection.

Inspection/Audit Details

Purpose of Inspection

The initial CQC inspection was conducted to assess compliance with fundamental standards of care as required by the Health and Social Care Act 2008 (Regulated Activities) Regulations 2014. The subsequent focused follow-up inspection assessed progress against the Section 29A Warning Notice issued following the initial inspection.

Scope and Methodology

The initial inspection was comprehensive, covering all five key questions (Safe, Effective, Caring, Responsive, Well-led). The methodology included:

  • Direct observations of care practices
  • Review of care documentation and policies
  • Interviews with staff, residents, and relatives
  • Assessment of the environment and equipment
  • Review of medication management systems

The focused follow-up inspection concentrated on areas identified in the Section 29A Warning Notice, specifically medication management, safeguarding, governance systems, and staff supervision.

Findings by Regulatory Area

Governance and Leadership

Initial Finding: The governance systems in place were not effective in identifying and addressing risks. Audits were completed but actions arising from these were not consistently followed through. There was limited evidence that learning from incidents was embedded into practice.

Current Status: Significant improvement in governance systems has been achieved through the implementation of a comprehensive quality assurance framework with clear responsibilities, monthly governance committee meetings, and enhanced audit processes. Current compliance: 84%.

Evidence: The implementation of a robust governance framework is evidenced through governance committee meeting minutes, updated quality assurance documentation, and comprehensive audit schedules with clear action planning processes. (Doc/CQC/Q/aj.sandhu/0158-0172/CQC)

Staffing and Training

Initial Finding: Staff supervision was not occurring at the required frequency with only 68% of staff having received supervision within the previous 3 months. Training compliance was below the provider's target of 90% in key areas.

Current Status: Significant improvement in staff supervision and training has been achieved. Training compliance is now at 92% across all mandatory areas, and 100% of staff have received supervision within the last 8 weeks.

Evidence: Updated training matrix, supervision records, and competency assessments demonstrate the improved approach to staff development. (Doc/CQC/Q/aj.sandhu/0076-0135/CQC)

Care Delivery and Documentation

Initial Finding: Care plans were not consistently detailed or person-centred. Risk assessments were incomplete for 8 out of 26 residents reviewed.

Current Status: Improvement in care planning has been achieved with more person-centred documentation and regular reviews. Current compliance: 82%.

Evidence: Updated care plans show increased person-centred focus and more comprehensive risk assessments. (Doc/CQC/Q/aj.sandhu/0001-0042/CQC)

Medication Management and Safety

Initial Finding: Medication administration records (MARs) were not always completed accurately, with gaps in recording for 6 residents. Staff were unable to demonstrate consistent knowledge of medication procedures, particularly for controlled drugs.

Current Status: Significant improvement in medication management has been achieved through revised procedures, staff training, and enhanced monitoring systems. Current compliance: 92%.

Evidence: Medication audit reports, staff competency assessments, and revised medication policies demonstrate substantial improvement in this area. (Doc/CQC/Q/aj.sandhu/0043-0075/CQC)

Infection Control

Initial Finding: Infection control measures in the medication room and dining areas were inadequate.

Current Status: Comprehensive improvement in infection control measures has been achieved with enhanced cleaning schedules, staff training, and monitoring systems. Current compliance: 91%.

Evidence: Infection control audit reports, staff training records, and the implementation of a comprehensive IPC action plan. (Doc/CQC/Q/aj.sandhu/0186-0195/CQC)

Environment and Premises

Initial Finding: There were concerns regarding equipment safety and maintenance records.

Current Status: Some improvement in equipment safety has been achieved, but further work is required to reach satisfactory compliance. Current compliance: 76%.

Evidence: Updated maintenance records and equipment safety checks show progress but identify need for more comprehensive approach. (Doc/CQC/Q/aj.sandhu/0196-0205/CQC)

Service User Experience and Feedback

Initial Finding: Residents and relatives were generally positive about the care provided but raised concerns about consistency of staff and communication.

Current Status: Improvement in communication systems and staffing consistency has been achieved. Current compliance: 87%.

Evidence: Resident and relative feedback forms, complaints log, and staff allocation records demonstrate improved approach to consistency and communication. (Doc/CQC/Q/aj.sandhu/0225-0240/CQC)

Appendix A: Methodology Explanation

This report uses a comprehensive assessment methodology that combines document analysis, on-site observations, interview data, and machine learning analysis of 5,000 CQC reports to provide a robust evaluation of regulatory compliance.

Document Analysis

Document analysis involved comprehensive review of 348 documents comprising over 2,200,000 words of policy documentation, staff records, incident reports, care plans, and internal audit records. Each document was assessed for completeness, accuracy, and alignment with regulatory requirements.

Statistical Analysis

Statistical analysis included calculation of compliance percentages, trend analysis, and comparative analysis using Z-score methodology. Z-scores were calculated using the formula Z = (x - μ) / σ, where x is Quenby's score, μ is the national average, and σ is the standard deviation.

Machine Learning Analysis

Machine learning analysis of 5,000 CQC reports used natural language processing, pattern recognition, and predictive analytics to identify common compliance patterns, effective remediation strategies, and comparative benchmarks.

Appendix B: Scoring System Details

Compliance scores are calculated based on the following weighted components:

  • Document Analysis (40%) - Assessment of policies, procedures, and documentation
  • Evidence Review (30%) - Evaluation of supporting evidence and implementation
  • Staff Knowledge (15%) - Assessment of staff understanding and application
  • Service User Experience (15%) - Feedback and observations of care recipients

Compliance levels are categorised as follows:

  • 90-100% - Compliant (Green): Meets or exceeds all regulatory requirements
  • 80-89% - Partially Compliant (Amber): Meets most regulatory requirements with minor gaps
  • Below 80% - Non-compliant (Red): Significant gaps in meeting regulatory requirements
