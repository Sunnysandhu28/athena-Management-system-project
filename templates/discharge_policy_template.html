Discharge Pack Policy and Procedure - CP02

1. Purpose

1.1 To ensure that the discharge of Residents is achieved in accordance with the values of Quenby Rest Home and there is a smooth transition between services that maintains continuity of care.

1.2 Key Question Quality Statements

1.3 Relevant Legislation

  • The Care Act 2014
  • Equality Act 2010
  • The Health and Social Care Act 2008 (Regulated Activities) Regulations 2014
  • Mental Capacity Act 2005
  • UK GDPR

2. Scope

Roles Affected:

  • Registered Manager
  • Other management
  • Administrator
  • Nurse Care staff

People Affected:

  • Residents

Stakeholders Affected:

  • Family
  • Advocates
  • Representatives
  • External health professionals
  • Local Authority
  • NHS

3. Objectives

Staff are clear on their roles and responsibilities in relation to discharge planning, transfer of care and delivering continuity of care during the process.

Quenby Rest Home supports discharge planning in a person-centred, individual, need by need way.

A blanket approach is not adopted when supporting Residents

4. Policy

Quenby Rest Home will endeavour to accommodate and meet the changing needs of Residents. Where this cannot be achieved, Residents will be supported and signposted to information to consider all options so that a decision to leave Quenby Rest Home will be informed and enabling. If appropriate, (i.e. not breaching data protection rules), all relevant information will be supplied to the alternative placement. The information provided must allow the placement to continue care and support.

5. Procedure

The full discharge pack (attached to this policy) can be used to ensure that all procedures are carried out where appropriate.

5.1 Discharge Notification

The following guidance should be followed:

  • Discharge forms should be commenced by care staff on the day of discharge
  • Once they have completed the top section, the form will be handed over to the administrator for completion in a timely manner
  • Once complete, the form will be filed with the Resident's records
  • Any issues identified with the completion of the form must be discussed with Mr Nithyanand Thirumalaisamy Navaneethakrishnan

Respite Care

At the point of commencement of the respite care placement, staff will be aware of the discharge date.
